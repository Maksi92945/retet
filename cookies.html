Cookies & Storage Policy

Effective: June 4, 2026

The short version

Alma uses only essential browser storage to keep you signed in and remember your UI preferences. No third-party cookies. No advertising or behavioural-tracking pixels. No analytics by default. You can clear it any time from your browser or with the button at the bottom of this page.

1. What "cookies" mean on Alma

Strictly speaking, Alma does not set any HTTP cookies at the moment. We use browser localStorage instead — small pieces of data kept in your own browser, never transmitted automatically with every request the way cookies are. They serve the same purpose (remembering your session and preferences) but are scoped to this site and easier for you to control.

We use the word "cookies" in this policy as a familiar umbrella term covering localStorage, sessionStorage, and any future use of actual HTTP cookies.

3. What we don't use

  • No third-party cookies.
  • No advertising cookies (no Facebook Pixel, no Google Ads, no retargeting tags).
  • No analytics by default (no Google Analytics, no Mixpanel, no Hotjar). If we ever add privacy-friendly analytics, we'll update this page and re-prompt you for consent.
  • No browser fingerprinting or canvas-tracking tricks.
  • No persistent cross-site identifiers.

4. Third-party services that may set their own storage

A few external scripts and embeds run on Alma. They are served from their own origins and may set their own storage under their domain, governed by their own policies:

  • Tailwind CDN (cdn.tailwindcss.com) — serves the CSS framework. Does not set tracking cookies.
  • Google Fonts (fonts.googleapis.com, fonts.gstatic.com) — loads the Geist and Instrument Serif fonts.
  • Lucide icons CDN (unpkg.com) — loads the SVG icon library.
  • AI provider (depending on configuration: Ollama on our infrastructure, or a cloud API like Groq / OpenAI / Anthropic) — receives the AI requests you send. See our Privacy Policy §4.

None of these services receive personal data about you from us beyond what is technically necessary to deliver their asset or process your AI request.

5. How to opt out

You have full control over Alma's browser storage:

  • Browser settings: open your browser's site settings for this domain and clear cookies / site data. You'll be signed out.
  • Incognito / private mode: nothing persists after you close the window.
  • Reset button at the bottom of this page — clears everything we wrote, immediately.

Heads-up: the items in section 2 are essential. If you block or clear them, you'll be signed out and your in-browser drafts, plans, voice profiles, etc. will be lost. Anything saved server-side (boards, account) stays — sign back in to access it.

6. Changes to this policy

If we ever add analytics, third-party widgets, or any optional storage, we will:

  • Update this page and bump the "Effective" date.
  • Re-show the consent banner so you can choose again.
  • Make optional categories truly optional — declining will not break the product.

7. Reset your cookie choice

Clears everything Alma stored in this browser: session, preferences, drafts, plans, your consent record. Server-side data (account, boards, subscription) is not affected.
